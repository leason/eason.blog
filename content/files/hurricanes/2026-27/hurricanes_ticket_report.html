With 41 games and 5 families, a perfectly equal split isn't possible — one family gets a 9th game while the rest get 8. This season that's Eason, and it's offset on purpose: their extra game is a weekday game, and they hold the fewest weekend games (4) of anyone. Every family lands within one game of every other on totals, weekend games, and weekday games. The 9th game can rotate to a different family each season.

How the split was made fair. Games were dealt out chronologically, always to the family that was next in line by count — weekends balanced first, then weekday games filling in to even the totals. A hard rule prevented any family from drawing two games in the same week (the busiest weeks have only three home games, so this was always possible). A final pass evened out the spacing so each family's nights land steadily across the season. Note: the Nov 14 vs. Seattle game is excluded — it's an official "home" game but is played in Helsinki, Finland, so it isn't part of the PNC Arena season-ticket package.
